Paper: PMC11650955.1.txt
Thermal Evidence:
- No temperature-related data was provided in the text.

Environmental Context:
- The study was conducted on a species housed in an enclosure comprising an indoor area and an outdoor grassy area.

Concise Summary:
This paper details the quantitative analysis of facial communication in a primate species, focusing on two distinct bared-teeth facial displays (BT and OMBT). The research utilized advanced techniques like MaqFACS and unsupervised clustering to analyze the morphology, stereotypy (using Levenshtein Distance), and context specificity of these signals across peaceful, playful, and agonistic social contexts. Key findings include the morphological separation of BT and OMBT, the observation that OMBT generally exhibits higher stereotypy than BT, and the identification of context-specific Action Units (AUs) that predict social states. The analysis emphasizes that facial expressions are highly plastic and context-dependent, suggesting that signal meaning is determined by the interplay between morphology, stereotypy, and social context, rather than being fixed to a single emotion or context.
