First detection of IncHI2 plasmid carrying blaNDM-5 in Escherichia coli from retail shellfish.

The increasing dissemination of carbapenemase-producing Enterobacterales (CPE) across multiple
ecological niches underscores a critical One Health challenge. Despite documentation of CPE
contamination in shrimp, the occurrence of CPE within broader aquatic food products remains
underexplored. In this study, 262 retail shellfish samples from farmers' markets were screened for
blaNDM-producing Gram-negative bacteria. Three Escherichia coli (ST6769, ST10, and ST3871) and five
Shewanella algae (unable to assign ST) isolates recovered from eight (3.1%) shellfish samples were
identified as carrying blaNDM. Hybrid assembly confirmed the presence of a blaNDM-5-carrying IncHI2
plasmid in one Escherichia coli isolate and blaNDM-1 on untypeable plasmids in Shewanella algae.
Both IncHI2 and untypeable plasmids shared highly similar structures with plasmids from various
sources in the GenBank database. This is the first study to report the detection of IncHI2 plasmids
carrying blaNDM-5 from retail shellfish. The widespread distribution of these plasmids may
facilitate the dissemination of blaNDM in aquatic food products.
