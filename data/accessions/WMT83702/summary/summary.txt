Paper: PMC10612066.1.txt
Thermal Evidence:
- Optimal growth temperature: 20–30 °C.
- Growth temperature range: 1–40 °C.
- Survival temperature range: Plaque formation observed across the entire tested range (1–40 °C), including at 1 and 3 °C.
- Incubation conditions: Tested at 1, 3, and 5–40 °C (in increments of 5 °C) for 1 week under aerobic conditions.
- Cold tolerance: Characterized as cold-active phages, exhibiting successful propagation at 1 °C.
- Thermal adaptation classification: Considered psychrotolerant bacteria.

Environmental Context:
- Habitat/Source: Sediment core samples from the continental shelf in the western Arctic Ocean.
- Extreme Environment Isolation: Yes, isolated from Arctic sediment.

Concise Summary:
Two novel phages were isolated from sediment core samples collected from the continental shelf in the western Arctic Ocean. The associated host bacteria were found to be psychrotolerant, exhibiting optimal growth between 20–30 °C but growing across a broad range of 1–40 °C. The phages themselves were characterized as cold-active, demonstrating successful plaque formation and propagation at very low temperatures, specifically 1 °C and 3 °C. Phage production was observed across the entire tested temperature range (1–40 °C), confirming their ability to function in cold environments typical of polar marine sediments. The study provides evidence of phage activity and survival in psychrophilic conditions.
