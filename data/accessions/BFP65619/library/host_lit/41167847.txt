Old yellow enzyme-catalyzed desymmetrizing desaturation.

Flavin-dependent old yellow enzymes (OYEs) have been widely employed as biocatalysts for the
stereoselective anti-hydrogenations of activated CC bonds through a "Ping-Pong" bi-bi mechanism.
Over the past decade, studies have expanded their reactivity to new-to-nature radical
transformations by exploiting their catalytic promiscuity and adaptability to protein engineering.
Recently, our group repurposed OYEs to enable asymmetric carbonyl desaturation by inverting their
native activity, addressing key limitations of traditional desaturation approaches-such as harsh
reaction conditions and inadequate stereochemical control. By directed evolution of an OYE from
Geobacillus kaustophilus (GkOYE), we achieved desymmetrizing desaturation of diverse cyclohexanones
to form the corresponding cyclohexenones featuring a remote quaternary stereogenic center under mild
conditions with excellent stereoselectivity. This chapter provides a comprehensive description of
the development of GkOYE-based desymmetrizing desaturation system, including high-throughput enzyme
engineering, protein expression, analytical and scalable reaction set-ups, offering a roadmap for
adapting OYEs to challenging desaturative transformations.
