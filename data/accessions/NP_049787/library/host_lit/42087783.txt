Structural and biochemical characterization of yeast Tcd enzymes installing the post-transcriptional modification ct6A in tRNA.

Post-transcriptional modifications near the anticodon of transfer ribonucleic acids (tRNAs) ensure
translation fidelity and accuracy. For instance, at position 37, the universally conserved and
essential nucleoside N6-threonylcarbamoyladenosine (t6A) supports decoding of ANN triplets. In some
organisms t6A is converted to cyclic t6A (ct6A), but only little is known about this ATP-dependent
reaction and the corresponding threonylcarbamoyladenosine dehydratases (Tcds). We here show that
yeast Tcds localize to the outer mitochondrial membrane and co-purify with tRNAs recognizing ANN
codons. Depending on the number of TCD genes in the genome, the proteins form V-shaped hetero- or
homodimers, of which at least one subunit binds and modifies tRNAs. The C-terminal, monomeric domain
shares similarities with Cas9-endonucleases and assists tRNA recognition, while the N-terminal
domain mediates dimerization and contains the active site. Structure-based mutagenesis and activity
assays imply that yeast Tcds lack a catalytic cysteine and do not covalently bind their substrate as
proposed for Escherichia coli TcdA.
