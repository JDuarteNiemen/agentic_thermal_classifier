Concise Summary:

Contamination simulations involved incubation at 10°C for 16 hours. The study focused on enzymatic efficacy at reduced operating temperatures (40–50°C), which is significantly milder than the high temperatures (60°C) used in conventional sanitation. The organism was isolated from bovine feces, and the research utilized these temperature parameters to demonstrate the potential for sustainable, low-temperature sanitation alternatives.
