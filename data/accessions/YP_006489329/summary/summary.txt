Paper: PMC5517026.1.txt
Thermal Evidence:
- Optimal activity observed at 45°C.
- Complete inactivation observed at 60°C.
- Thermal stability (Tm) determined to be 81°C.
- The enzyme exhibits thermostable characteristics despite being isolated from a psychrophilic source.

Environmental Context:
- Isolated from Arctic marine sediment.

Concise Summary:
The enzyme was isolated from a psychrophilic bacterium found in Arctic marine sediment. While the organism is psychrophilic, the purified enzyme exhibits notable thermostability, with optimal activity measured at 45°C and a denaturation temperature ($T_m$) of 81°C. The enzyme's stability is attributed to its high-order octameric structure, suggesting structural adaptations that confer thermostability beyond typical psychrophilic enzyme characteristics.

Concise Summary:

Its growth profile indicates adaptation to cold environments, with a reported growth temperature range of approximately -1 °C to 10 °C and an optimal growth temperature of 8 °C. The study details the structural characterization of a capsular polysaccharide (CPS) produced under these cold conditions, noting that the polysaccharide itself was tested for cryoprotective activity, although the specific CPS analyzed showed low ice recrystallization inhibition activity compared to other polysaccharides from the same source grown at 4 °C. The overall context emphasizes the organism's ability to survive and grow in low-temperature environments.


Paper: PMC11858272.1.txt
Thermal Evidence:
- Optimal growth temperature: 8 °C (for one strain).
- Growth temperature range: One strain has a range of −12 to 19 °C; another has a range of −8 to 25 °C; and a third has a range of −1 to 25 °C.

Environmental Context:

- Extreme Environment Isolation: Yes, strains were isolated from cold and saline environments.
- Explicit Classification: One strain is described as obligately psychrophilic; the other two are described as psychrotolerant.


Paper: PMC6390093.1.txt
Thermal Evidence:
- Maximum activity observed at 40°C for the hydrolysis of pNP-TMP.
- Approximately 50% relative activity maintained at 10°C.
- Activity dropped sharply at temperatures exceeding 50°C.
- Thermal stability assessment showed near-complete denaturation at ~50°C.
- The melting temperature ($T_m$) was determined to be 36.3°C.
- The enzyme exhibits properties typical of cold-active enzymes.

Environmental Context:
- Isolated from Arctic marine sediments.
- The organism is classified as an obligate psychrophile.

Concise Summary:
The enzyme studied originates from an obligate psychrophile isolated from Arctic marine sediments. Biochemical assays demonstrate that the enzyme exhibits cold-active characteristics, with optimal activity observed at 40°C. The enzyme retains significant activity (approx. 50%) at 10°C, but activity drops sharply above 50°C, and the thermal stability analysis revealed a melting temperature ($T_m$) of 36.3°C, consistent with psychrophilic adaptation.
